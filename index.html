Research Analyst – Built predictive models to reduce no-shows and improve fan engagement using Python and Power BI

January 2025 – Present

At University of South Carolina Athletics Department, I leveraged Python and machine learning to analyze ticket sales and attendance data, building predictive models to estimate the likelihood of fan no-shows. I identified key drivers of attendance such as opponent strength, ticket type, and purchasing behavior. Reducing “No-Shows” at UofSC Athletic Events

Not all ticket purchasers attend the games, leading to “no-shows” and a decline in true attendance. These no-shows result in empty seats, a diminished atmosphere, lost concession revenue, and a weaker overall fan experience. UofSC Athletics sought to identify the factors driving attendance behavior and reduce future no-shows.

To support this goal, a team and I developed a predictive model (see the Model Portfolio tab for more details) to estimate the likelihood that a ticket holder would attend a given game. The model was implemented directly into our dashboard, where we visualized high-risk tickets on a geographic heat map.

This approach allowed us to:

  • Identify characteristics of fans most likely to skip games
  • Quantify the key drivers of attendance
  • Target specific ticket holders for engagement strategies

Ultimately, this project enabled data-driven decision-making to improve attendance and enhance the in-stadium experience.

Understanding Traffic Crashes in the City of Chicago

Traffic crashes are a major concern for public safety, infrastructure planning, and equitable resource allocation. This dashboard was created to identify the when, where, and why of crashes, providing insights that help develop strategies to decrease both the frequency and severity of traffic incidents.

To achieve this, I created a series of visualizations that analyze crash trends over time, categorize crash types and severity, and display crash density through an interactive heat map by police beat. Users can apply filters to examine crashes based on severity, time frame, and the presence of nearby traffic control devices. These filters provide deeper insights into contributing factors and high-risk areas, allowing for a more targeted approach to understanding and mitigating traffic incidents.

Identifying Skin Cancer Detection - ISIC 2024

This project addresses the critical need for accessible skin cancer diagnostics in underserved regions by developing a deep learning-based detection system that works with smartphone-quality images. Leveraging the ISIC SLICE-3D dataset and state-of-the-art EfficientNet architectures, we evaluate the impact of image quality on classification performance. Through transfer learning from high-resolution ISIC 2019 data, we demonstrate how advanced AI can adapt to low-resource environments without compromising diagnostic accuracy, paving the way for broader, more equitable access to early skin cancer detection tools.
